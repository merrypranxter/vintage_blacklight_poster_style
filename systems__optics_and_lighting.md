# Optics and Lighting

## Lighting Philosophy

This style does not simulate ordinary lighting.
It simulates emission, aura, fluorescence, and pigment excitation.

## Core Light Modes

### 1. Rim Glow
A bright contour wrapping the subject.

Use for:
- silhouette separation
- eye-catching edge clarity
- black background punch

### 2. Bloom Halo
A larger soft haze around bright zones.

Use for:
- aura
- perceived luminosity
- mystical atmosphere

### 3. Internal Radiance
Glow appearing to come from inside forms.

Use for:
- eyes
- crystals
- moons
- magical symbols
- mushrooms
- celestial skin

### 4. Accent Spark
Tiny hard highlights.

Use for:
- star points
- fang glints
- eye reflections
- wet edges
- crystal tips

### 5. Atmospheric Field Glow
Diffuse chromatic clouds in the surrounding dark.

Use for:
- depth
- dreaminess
- composition framing

## Hierarchy Rules

- Primary subject gets strongest glow
- Secondary motifs get controlled support glow
- Background glow should never overpower the focal figure
- White should be reserved for the most energetic points

## Edge Logic

Use a mixture of:

- soft aura edges
- selective hard contour accents
- bloom tails
- feathered transitions

## Shadow Logic

Shadows are chromatic, theatrical, and stylized.

Common shadow hues:
- purple
- blue
- magenta
- deep green in rare toxic schemes

## Do Not Overdo

Too much bloom destroys poster readability.
Keep glow layered, not fogged-out.
