# Common Failure Modes

## 1. Too Much Detail Everywhere

Problem:
The poster loses across-room punch.

Fix:
- enlarge focal subject
- reduce secondary clutter
- keep detail concentrated in 1–2 key zones

## 2. Glow Without Hierarchy

Problem:
Everything glows equally and nothing matters.

Fix:
- define primary glow, secondary glow, accent glow
- keep some areas nearly unlit

## 3. Weak Black

Problem:
Background drifts to dark blue, purple, or fog-gray and loses contrast force.

Fix:
- restore true black zones
- use colored haze selectively, not everywhere

## 4. Overly Clean Digital Finish

Problem:
Image feels like vector neon wallpaper instead of painted blacklight art.

Fix:
- add softness
- add airbrush texture
- vary edge hardness
- add slight pigment bloom irregularity

## 5. Wrong Subject Choice

Problem:
Subject feels mundane, modern, or emotionally flat.

Fix:
- choose iconically loaded subjects
- choose dream-symbolic or fantasy-charged content

## 6. Color Chaos

Problem:
All colors compete equally and the image turns into fluorescent soup.

Fix:
- choose dominant hue family
- choose support hue family
- use 1–2 accent pops
- let black separate them

## 7. Generic Psychedelic Sludge

Problem:
Image feels like random swirls and rainbow smoke with no poster identity.

Fix:
- restore figure or symbol anchor
- use framing
- use hierarchy
- sharpen silhouette
