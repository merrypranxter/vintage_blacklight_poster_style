# Visual DNA

## Core DNA Components

The visual DNA of vintage blacklight posters can be broken into the following recurring layers:

1. **Void Base**
  - deep black or near-black field
  - minimal environmental realism
  - negative space used as glow amplifier

2. **Fluorescent Palette**
  - hot magenta
  - electric blue
  - ultraviolet purple
  - acid green
  - hot orange
  - yellow flare
  - occasional white plasma accents

3. **Glow Envelope**
  - halos
  - soft feathered edges
  - bloom zones
  - emissive contour logic
  - spectral aura wrapping

4. **Central Icon**
  - a wolf, cat, wizard, woman, dragon, mushroom cluster, moon scene, or similar
  - large and readable from a distance

  - often centered or near-centered

5. **Dream Symbol Layer**
  - stars
  - moons
  - lightning
  - crystals
  - smoke
  - galaxies
  - mandala-like radiance
  - zodiac motifs
  - water, mist, cosmic clouds

6. **Airbrush or Velvet Modeling**
  - smooth gradients
  - impossible color shadows
  - glamorized and soft transitions
  - plush darkness, not flat digital black

7. **Highlight Accent Layer**
  - sparkling points
  - white or cyan pinpricks
  - sharp contrast pops
  - eye highlights, fang highlights, crystal tips, moon edges

## DNA Priority Ranking

If you only preserve five things, preserve these:

1. black void dominance
2. fluorescent palette intensity
3. glow hierarchy
4. iconic central subject
5. mystical or fantasy poster logic

## DNA Signature Sentence

A vintage blacklight poster is a fluorescent dream icon floating in black velvet, painted with
impossible light.
