# Artifact Logic

## Why Artifacts Matter

Vintage blacklight posters often feel compelling because they are not perfectly clean.
They carry visual residue from printing, airbrush diffusion, pigment spread, and analog
imperfection.

## Useful Artifact Types

### 1. Soft Overspray
Adds analog realism to airbrushed edges.

### 2. Glow Irregularity
Makes fluorescence feel pigment-based rather than software-perfect.

### 3. Tiny Registration Drift
Can be used very subtly in digital homage versions.

### 4. Speckle / Dust
Useful for starfield, glitter, or print texture simulation.

### 5. Gradient Noise
Prevents digital smoothness from becoming sterile.

## How Much to Use

Use lightly.
Too much artifacting turns tribute into fake distress or grime aesthetics.

## Shader Translation

Artifacts can be mapped to:
- low-amplitude noise
- edge diffusion variation
- tiny sparkle stochastic distribution
- slight chroma separation in highlights
