# Palette System

## Palette Philosophy

The palette must feel chemically exaggerated, not naturally observed.

The black field acts as the real neutral.
All colors should be selected in relation to how violently they punch against black.

## Core Palette Families

### Family A: Ultraviolet Dream
- hot magenta
- violet
- ultraviolet purple
- electric blue
- cyan
- white accents

### Family B: Toxic Aurora
- acid green
- fluorescent yellow
- cyan

- deep blue
- orange sparks

### Family C: Solar Heat Bloom
- hot pink
- orange
- yellow
- purple shadow
- electric blue accents

### Family D: Lunar Mystic
- cyan
- deep blue
- violet
- magenta
- pale white glow

## Palette Roles

### Dominant Hue
Used for the main emotional identity.
Examples:
- violet
- magenta
- electric blue

### Secondary Hue
Supports form separation and gradient movement.
Examples:
- cyan
- purple
- acid green

### Accent Hue
Used sparingly for retinal shocks.
Examples:
- yellow
- orange
- white
- hot pink

### Dark Support
Usually black, near-black, or extremely deep blue-purple

## Color Behavior Rules

1. Never let every hue occupy equal area.
2. Use black as separator.
3. Use white sparingly for ionized highlights.
4. Avoid muddy middle saturation.
5. Prefer exaggerated complementary clashes.
6. Use gradients theatrically, not realistically.

## Suggested Ratios

- 45–65% black / near-black
- 20–30% dominant glow hues
- 10–20% secondary hues
- 2–8% accent hits

## Blacklight-Safe Pairings

- magenta + cyan + violet
- electric blue + acid green + yellow
- hot pink + orange + purple
- cyan + white + blue + magenta

## Avoid

- beige
- earth tones
- muted olive
- natural skin logic unless heavily stylized
- flat gray unless used as hidden transition support
