# Composition Grammar

## Poster Composition Rule

The image should function first as a wall icon, second as an illustration.

## Common Layout Structures

### 1. Central Icon
A large face, animal, moon, mushroom cluster, or symbol sits near center.

Best for:
- classic poster feel
- maximum readability
- strong focal impact

### 2. Vertical Totem
Elements stacked along a vertical axis.

Best for:
- goddess figures
- wolves and moons
- mushrooms and portals
- layered fantasy symbolism

### 3. Framed Portal
A subject inside smoke, vines, stars, or radiance rings.

Best for:
- cosmic windows
- dream scenes
- magical gateways

### 4. Split Sky / Ground Fantasy
Foreground subject with glowing horizon or moon field.

Best for:
- wolves
- castles
- unicorns
- forest fantasy scenes

### 5. Face + Symbol Orbit
A face or head surrounded by moons, stars, zodiac symbols, feathers, crystals.

Best for:
- celestial feminine posters
- mystical portraiture

## Composition Rules

1. One major focal subject.
2. Two or three secondary support elements max.
3. Strong silhouette.
4. Outer field stays mostly dark.
5. Glow increases toward focal center.
6. Decorative motifs frame, not flatten.

## Symmetry Guidance

Symmetry is allowed, but should not become sterile.
Use:
- soft symmetry
- symbolic balance
- asymmetrical atmospheric variation

## Depth Strategy

Depth is usually shallow-to-moderate.
This is wall-art depth, not cinematic realism.

Depth layers:
- foreground icon
- aura layer
- symbolic midground
- dark field / cosmic background
