# Glow Stack Model

## Overview

A recommended four-layer glow architecture for blacklight poster simulation.

## Layer 1: Core Form

Purpose:
Defines the subject silhouette and internal color masses.

Traits:
- strongest form readability
- least blur
- highest composition importance

## Layer 2: Rim Emission

Purpose:
Separates the subject from blackness.

Traits:
- bright contour
- medium intensity
- localized to edges and important feature lines

## Layer 3: Bloom Halo

Purpose:
Creates perceived luminosity.

Traits:
- soft blur
- larger radius
- lower opacity
- color inherited from core hue family

## Layer 4: Aura Field

Purpose:
Extends mystical atmosphere beyond the form.

Traits:
- very soft
- broad radius
- sparse and patchy rather than uniform
- may mix with atmospheric haze

## Accent Layer: Spark / Highlight

Purpose:
Micro-contrast energy points.

Traits:
- tiny white or cyan hits
- eye glints, star points, crystal tips, fang edges

## Suggested Intensity Logic

- core form: 1.0
- rim emission: 0.7–1.1
- bloom halo: 0.2–0.5
- aura field: 0.05–0.2
- accent spark: brief peaks, small area only

## Important Rule

Do not blur the whole image equally.
Glow must be hierarchical or the poster loses its punch.
