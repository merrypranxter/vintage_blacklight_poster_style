# Boundaries and Anti-Drift

## What This Style Is

- fluorescent
- black-backed
- painterly or airbrushed
- fantasy / cosmic / symbolic
- wall-poster iconic
- glow-structured

## What This Style Is Not

- minimalism
- flat corporate neon
- daylight realism
- matte natural landscape painting
- cyberpunk cityscape by default
- vaporwave by default
- synthwave grid art by default
- cute children’s stationery art
- glossy plastic retail rainbow art

## Drift Risks

### Drift: Generic Neon
Symptoms:
- lots of bright color, but no blacklight logic
- glows everywhere with no structure
- looks like nightclub branding

Correction:
- reintroduce void
- enforce central icon
- use softer pigment-like glow
- reduce modern graphic polish

### Drift: Synthwave
Symptoms:
- sun grids
- chrome cars
- wireframe mountains
- magenta-cyan sunset trope overload

Correction:
- swap geometric retro-future motifs for mystical/fantasy motifs
- increase smoke, stars, animals, moons, aura fields
- reduce hard 80s tech tropes

### Drift: Lisa Frank / Stickerland
Symptoms:
- too cute
- too glossy
- too all-over bright
- black field weakened
- charm replaces mystery

Correction:
- deepen black field
- increase mystical charge
- add atmospheric shadow
- reduce toy-like cleanliness

### Drift: Generic AI Sacred Geometry
Symptoms:
- all symmetry, no poster identity
- mandala sludge
- no focal hierarchy
- no subject charisma

Correction:
- restore subject-centered composition
- reduce symmetrical filler
- add figurative or symbolic anchor

## Anti-Drift Rules

1. Preserve black dominance.
2. Preserve subject hierarchy.
3. Preserve glow structure.
4. Preserve fantasy/occult/cosmic tone.
5. Preserve analog softness.
6. Avoid modern UI-clean rendering.
7. Avoid default retro clichés unless hybridizing on purpose.
